Chronology of Islamic Civilization

632 CE
Death of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ
Start of Rashidun Caliphate and rapid Islamic expansion under Abu Bakr, Umar, Uthman, and Ali.
661–750
Umayyad Caliphate
Capital in Damascus. Expansion into North Africa, Spain, and Central Asia.
750–1258
Abbasid Caliphate
Golden Age centered in Baghdad. Flourishing of science, medicine, philosophy, and culture.
1258
Mongol Sack of Baghdad
End of centralized Abbasid rule. Fragmentation of Islamic authority.
1300s–1600s
Gunpowder Empires
Rise of the Ottoman, Safavid, and Mughal empires with military and cultural dominance.
1700s–1800s
Decline of Empires
Weakening of Islamic empires due to internal issues and European colonialism.
19th–20th Century
Colonialism & Nationalism
Rise of nationalist movements, decline of colonial power, push for independence in Muslim lands.
1924
Abolition of the Caliphate
Ottoman Caliphate formally ended by Mustafa Kemal Atatürk. Transition to modern nation-states.
Modern Era
Nation States & Global Islam
Emergence of modern Muslim-majority countries and ongoing global Islamic contributions.
